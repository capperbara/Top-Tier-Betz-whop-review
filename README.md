## Types of Sports Bets Covered

  

Top Tier Betz covers all major bet types that are popular in legal sports betting markets:

  

### Moneyline and Point Spread Bets

  

These are the bread and butter picks you'll see daily.

  

Moneyline bets require your team to win outright, while point spread bets involve betting against the spread where favorites "give" points and underdogs "take" points.

  

For example, if the Lakers are -3.5 against the Warriors, they need to win by 4 or more points for the bet to cash.

  

### Over/Under Totals

  

Total bets focus on the combined score of both teams.

  

If an NBA game has a total of 215.5 points, you're betting whether the final combined score goes over or under that number.

  

### Parlay Combinations

  

The server provides both conservative and aggressive parlay options.

  

Parlays combine multiple bets where all selections must win for the bet to pay out.

  

The potential payouts are much higher, but so is the risk since one wrong pick kills the entire bet.

  

### Proposition Bets

  

These are specific outcome bets not related to the final score.

  

Examples include betting on how many points LeBron James will score or whether there will be a safety in an NFL game.

  

Prop bets often offer great value when you have inside knowledge about player matchups.

  

### Teaser Bets

  

Teasers are modified parlays that give you better point spreads in exchange for lower payouts.

  

For instance, you might move a 3-point favorite to +3.5 points in your favor, but the payout is reduced.

  

### Futures Bets

  

Long-term wagers on season outcomes like Super Bowl winners or MVP awards.

  

These bets tie up your money for months but can offer huge payouts early in the season.

  

### Live Betting

  

In-play betting on games as they happen.

  

The server provides real-time analysis for adjusting strategies during games.

  

Live betting has become increasingly popular since it allows you to react to game flow and momentum shifts.

## Sports Betting Legality and Compliance

  

Before joining any sports betting community, you must understand the legal landscape.

  

The Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act of 1992 (PASPA) previously made sports betting illegal in most US states.

  

However, the Supreme Court struck down PASPA in Murphy v. National Collegiate Athletic Association in 2018.

  

Since then, over $20 billion has been wagered legally on sports in the United States as of 2020.

  

Currently, 37 states plus Washington D.C. and Puerto Rico have operational legal sports betting.

  

30 states allow online sports betting, while 8 states restrict betting to physical sportsbooks only.

  

**Always verify that sports betting is legal in your jurisdiction before participating in any betting activities.**
